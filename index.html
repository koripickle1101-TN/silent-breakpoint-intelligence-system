Signature Case Study

Prior Authorization Delay

Problem

A prior authorization request remains unresolved because no one owns the aging queue after submission.

Upstream Origin

The failure starts in authorization workflow design where no escalation trigger is assigned.

Where It Became Visible

The issue appears later as delayed care rescheduled service and reimbursement risk.

Silent Breakpoint Category

Delayed approval manual escalation and operational visibility gap.

Operational Impact

Patients wait staff recheck manually revenue stalls and leadership sees the issue too late.

Recommended Fix

Create an authorization aging tracker with assigned owners service date priority and 24 48 72 hour escalation rules.

Metric To Track

Average authorization aging days and percent escalated before service date.

Leadership Insight

Authorization problems are not only payer problems. They are workflow visibility problems.
